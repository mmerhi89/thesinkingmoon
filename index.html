The Sinking Moon

The Baltic waves at night broke the sounds of desolate darkness in preparation to swallow the sinking moon deep into the belly of the earth. The moon once pale and anemic, is now covered behind a red veil. As the moon descends to meet the water, the magnetic pull of the deep sea draws its power from rubbing salt against open wounds. The more the moon struggled, the more it sank to its demise. Like a ship-wrecked from within, the moon is cleansed through a cathartic experience among the currents.

THINKING PROCESSES

Thinking Processes


As I listened to the chimes of the night, I stared at the soul of the sinking moon, and felt envious of its kindled ability to transform and survive under such darkness. I was compelled to touch the moon, but if I did my internal fire would crumble. It was not my moon touch, neither was it mine to sink. Although in that very moment I knew I would endure the same fate in due time. Just like the moon, we go through different processes of cleansing, healing and acceptance.

Catharisis /kəˈθɑːsɪs/ noun

Definition

The process of releasing, and thereby providing relief from strong or repressed emotions.

Meaning

According to Aristotle's Poetics, catharsis is when an indivdual experiences fear and a suffering that seems to be of their own. Through a series of inexplicable events, an emotional relief achieves balance and harmony. This transformation is a cleanse for the soul, and a bodily transportation purifying one's pain.

Personal Experience

It has taken me 7 months to express the phenomenon I saw while crossing the Baltic Sea. I did not realise then what I realise now, that there was an imbalance. When the moon sank within 27 minutes, I knew one day my conflicted unconscious will surface when the tide rises. I yearned for the light to guide me, but instead I caught myself on fire. This burning sensation helped me achieve an emotional equilibrium. With the right time and distance, this cleanse has given me a new perspective and clarity.
